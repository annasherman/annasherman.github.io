My Work

My latest and favorite venture! A drag and drop workout-builder with ambitions to house a crowdsourced weightlifting API. This app uses MongoDB, Express, Angular, and Node, as well as RubaXa's Sortable.js library. I aimed for an unobtrusive UI, with no page reloads happening during workout edits and silent, behind-the-scenes ordering. Future plans include build out with videos & fitness resources.

This is a joint effort between myself and front-end ninja Simran Khosla. We noticed a lack of user-friendly flashcard/studying applications and decided to build our own. We envisioned is as a "learn anything" app, allowing users to study for a specific exam or to browse public decks without account creation. This app is built in Node & Express, with Handlebars as our view engine and a fully custom front-end using JQuery UI.

This is a travel wishlist app built by myself and classmate Sam Groesch. We wanted an interactive way to keep track of places we had been and places we wanted to go. Originally called "Project Too Real", we are working on expanding the app to bring up World Bank data on click, allowing users to compare country data side by side to better quantify and understand differences in privilege and standard of living. This app is built in Sinatra.

This was my first project at General Assembly! It is a matching game and a first foray into client side JavaScript. I hope to use it educationally anywhere that memorization plays a key role -- for example, foreign language vocabulary. For now, staying true to my fitness theme, it houses some common lifts. Stay tuned -- this app is in line for a facelift.
